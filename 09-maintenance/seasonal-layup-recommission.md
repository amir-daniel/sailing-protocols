# Seasonal — Lay-Up & Recommission

- **Category:** Maintenance
- **Style:** READ–DO
- **When to use:** End of season (lay-up) and start of season (recommission).
- **Crew:** Owner / yard.

> Killer items are shown in **bold**.

## Lay-up (end of season)
### Engine & systems
- [ ] Change engine oil and filter; run to circulate before storage
- [ ] **Winterise the cooling system (antifreeze) if freezing is possible**
- [ ] Fill or treat the fuel tank to prevent condensation/diesel bug
- [ ] Remove and store/charge batteries; keep topped up over winter
- [ ] Drain freshwater system and add non-toxic antifreeze

### Hull, rig & sails
- [ ] Haul out; pressure-wash and inspect the hull, keel, rudder
- [ ] Check anodes; note antifouling needs
- [ ] Remove sails; wash, dry, inspect, store dry
- [ ] Unstep or inspect the mast/rig as per your schedule
- [ ] **Ventilate the interior; prevent damp/mould** (open lockers, use dehumidifier)

### Safety gear
- [ ] Note service/expiry dates: liferaft, flares, EPIRB, extinguishers, lifejackets

## Recommission (start of season)
- [ ] Reverse the winterisation (coolant, water system, fuel)
- [ ] Refit charged batteries; test all electrics and electronics
- [ ] Antifoul; replace anodes; check seacocks operate freely
- [ ] **Full rig check before stepping/tensioning; inspect all terminals**
- [ ] Bend on sails; check running rigging
- [ ] Service/replace out-of-date safety gear; **liferaft serviced**
- [ ] Engine: check levels/belts/impeller; test-run and confirm cooling water
- [ ] Sea trial before the first real passage

## Notes & gotchas
- Freeze damage (cracked heat exchanger/block) is the classic expensive lay-up mistake — winterise the cooling system properly.
- Book liferaft and flare servicing early; slots fill up before the season.
