# Weekly Checks

- **Category:** Maintenance
- **Style:** DO–CONFIRM
- **When to use:** Weekly on a long cruise/passage, or before each weekend of use.
- **Crew:** Skipper or engineer.

> Killer items are shown in **bold**.

## Engine & mechanical
- [ ] Check/clean the **raw-water strainer**
- [ ] Inspect belts closely; check tension
- [ ] Check for fuel/oil/coolant leaks and top up as needed
- [ ] Drain any water from the fuel pre-filter/separator
- [ ] Grease/lubricate as per the engine schedule

## Electrical
- [ ] Inspect battery terminals; clean and protect
- [ ] Top up (flooded) batteries; check secure and ventilated
- [ ] Test all navigation and interior lights

## Rig & sails
- [ ] Detailed rig check: **swage terminals, spreader tips, clevis/split pins**
- [ ] Check reefing lines, blocks, and cars run freely
- [ ] Inspect sails for seam/UV damage; note repairs needed

## Hull & plumbing
- [ ] **Exercise and inspect all seacocks; check hose clamps**
- [ ] Test bilge pumps (manual + electric) and float switch
- [ ] Check freshwater system and pump; look for leaks

## Safety
- [ ] Inspect flares/EPIRB/PLB in-date status
- [ ] Test the VHF (radio check) and DSC
- [ ] Check gas system for leaks; inspect hose date

## Notes & gotchas
- The raw-water strainer and fuel pre-filter are the two things that quietly clog
  and stop the engine — keep them clean.
