# Electrical / Battery Management

- **Category:** Systems & engine
- **Style:** DO–CONFIRM
- **When to use:** Daily on passage and when managing power.
- **Crew:** Skipper or engineer.

> Killer items are shown in **bold**.

## Monitor
- [ ] Check battery **state of charge / voltage** (house and start banks)
- [ ] **Keep the engine-start battery isolated and protected** — never run it flat
- [ ] Note daily energy use vs. charging (engine, solar, wind, shore)
- [ ] Check charging is working when the engine runs (voltage rises)

## Manage consumption
- [ ] Prioritise essential loads: nav lights, instruments, VHF, autopilot
- [ ] Reduce non-essential draw when charge is low (fridge, inverter, cabin lights)
- [ ] Plan engine-charging runs before the house bank gets too low

## Safety
- [ ] **Keep battery terminals tight, clean, and protected** (loose = heat/fire risk)
- [ ] Ensure batteries are secured and ventilated (gassing)
- [ ] Know how to switch banks and how the isolators are wired
- [ ] Keep a means to start the engine if the house bank fails (emergency parallel)

## Notes & gotchas
- Protect the start battery above all — being unable to start the engine offshore is serious.
- A loose/corroded terminal mimics a "flat battery" and can overheat; check connections first.
