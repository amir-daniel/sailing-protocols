# Engine Shutdown

- **Category:** Systems & engine
- **Style:** DO–CONFIRM
- **When to use:** Stopping the engine after use.
- **Crew:** Skipper or engineer.

> Killer items are shown in **bold**.

## Before stopping
- [ ] Gear lever in **neutral**
- [ ] Let the engine idle for a minute or two to cool (especially after hard running / turbo)

## Stop
1. [ ] Operate the stop control until the engine stops
2. [ ] Turn the ignition off; **confirm the alarm buzzer stops** (key fully off)
3. [ ] **Consider closing the raw-water seacock** if leaving the boat / rough weather (avoid siphoning / flooding)

## After
- [ ] Turn off engine battery/isolator if leaving the boat (per your setup)
- [ ] Note running hours in the log
- [ ] Quick visual: any new leaks, drips, or belt dust?

## Notes & gotchas
- Leaving the ignition partially on flattens the battery and can damage the alternator — confirm it's fully off.
- If you close the raw-water seacock, **tag the helm/key so no one starts it dry**.
