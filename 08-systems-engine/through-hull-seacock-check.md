# Through-Hull / Seacock Check

- **Category:** Systems & engine
- **Style:** DO–CONFIRM
- **When to use:** Before departure, periodically on passage, and before leaving the boat.
- **Crew:** Skipper or engineer.

> Killer items are shown in **bold**. Every seacock is a potential hole in the boat.

## Know your seacocks
- [ ] **Have a diagram/list of every through-hull and what it serves**
- [ ] Confirm a **soft-wood bung is tied at (or near) each seacock**

## Inspection
- [ ] Each seacock operates freely (open/close fully) — exercise them regularly
- [ ] **No weeping, corrosion, or cracks at the fitting, hose, or clamps**
- [ ] Double hose clamps present, tight, and not rusted
- [ ] Hoses supple, not perished, ends secure

## Operating state
- [ ] Engine raw-water intake **OPEN before starting**
- [ ] Heads inlet/outlet set correctly; **closed when leaving the boat / offshore in rough weather**
- [ ] Log/depth transducer seals sound
- [ ] Sink/cockpit drains clear and operating

## Notes & gotchas
- A failed skin fitting is a fast route to sinking — the tied bung is your instant fix.
- Seacocks seize if never exercised; operate them regularly so they work when needed.
