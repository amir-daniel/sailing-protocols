# Heads (Toilet) Operation & Blockage

- **Category:** Systems & engine
- **Style:** DO–CONFIRM
- **When to use:** Briefing guests; clearing a blocked marine toilet.
- **Crew:** Anyone; skipper for blockages.

> Killer items are shown in **bold**. "If you didn't eat it, don't flush it."

## Normal operation (manual pump heads)
- [ ] Inlet/flush seacock **open**, outlet seacock **open** before use
- [ ] Lever to "flush" (wet): pump to add water and clear the bowl
- [ ] Lever to "dry": pump to empty the bowl without adding water
- [ ] **Nothing goes in that hasn't been eaten first** — no wipes, sanitary items, excess paper
- [ ] Leave the bowl empty (dry) and pumped through after use

## Leaving the boat / offshore in rough weather
- [ ] **Close the inlet and outlet seacocks** (prevents back-flooding/siphoning)
- [ ] Brief crew that seacocks must be reopened before next use

## Clearing a blockage
1. [ ] Stop pumping against resistance — **don't force it** (you'll burst a hose/joint)
2. [ ] Check the **outlet seacock is actually open** (the #1 cause)
3. [ ] Check the vented loop / anti-siphon valve isn't clogged
4. [ ] Dismantle the pump; clear the blockage (gloves; expect mess)
5. [ ] Check the outlet hose for **calcium build-up** (common long-term cause)

## Notes & gotchas
- Most "blockages" are simply a closed outlet seacock — check that first.
- Forcing a blocked pump splits hoses/joints and can cause a slow flood — stop and diagnose.
