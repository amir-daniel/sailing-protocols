# NATO Phonetic Alphabet & Numbers

- **Category:** Radio protocols
- **Style:** Reference
- **When to use:** Spelling names, callsigns, and letters clearly over VHF.

## Phonetic alphabet
| Letter | Word | Letter | Word |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| A | Alfa | N | November |
| B | Bravo | O | Oscar |
| C | Charlie | P | Papa |
| D | Delta | Q | Quebec |
| E | Echo | R | Romeo |
| F | Foxtrot | S | Sierra |
| G | Golf | T | Tango |
| H | Hotel | U | Uniform |
| I | India | V | Victor |
| J | Juliett | W | Whiskey |
| K | Kilo | X | X-ray |
| L | Lima | Y | Yankee |
| M | Mike | Z | Zulu |

## Numbers (spoken)
| Digit | Spoken | Digit | Spoken |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 0 | Zero | 5 | Five |
| 1 | One | 6 | Six |
| 2 | Two | 7 | Seven |
| 3 | Three | 8 | Eight |
| 4 | Four | 9 | Nine |

- Decimal point spoken as "**decimal**" (e.g. Ch 16 = "one six").
- Speak digits individually: 156 = "one five six", not "one hundred fifty-six".

## Notes & gotchas
- Spell your boat name phonetically on first contact if it's unusual.
- Say channel numbers digit by digit ("channel seven two", not "seventy-two").
