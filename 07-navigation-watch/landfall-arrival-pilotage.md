# Landfall & Arrival Pilotage

- **Category:** Navigation & watch
- **Style:** READ–DO
- **When to use:** Approaching a harbour/anchorage after a passage.
- **Crew:** Skipper/navigator + lookout.

> Killer items are shown in **bold**. Plan the entry *before* you arrive, ideally in daylight.

## Plan before arrival
- [ ] Study the harbour: **entrance, depths, tidal access window, hazards, leading marks**
- [ ] Note the **tidal height/stream** for your arrival time (enough depth? fair tide?)
- [ ] Identify buoyage/leading lines and a clear **transit** to run in on
- [ ] Note port VHF channel, hours, and any traffic signals
- [ ] Have a **pilotage plan / sketch**: courses, marks, clearing bearings, danger areas
- [ ] Plan a **safe abort/turn-back** if it doesn't look right

## On approach
- [ ] Fix position; confirm you're identifying the **correct** marks (verify, don't assume)
- [ ] **Call the marina/harbour** for a berth and instructions
- [ ] Reduce sail / prepare the engine in good time; **confirm cooling water**
- [ ] Post a lookout; watch depth against your tidal calculation
- [ ] Use clearing bearings/transits to stay in safe water

## Enter & berth
- [ ] Rig fenders and lines **both sides** before entering (see coming-alongside)
- [ ] Follow buoyage; give way per COLREGs and local rules; keep to starboard
- [ ] Proceed at slow, controlled speed; be ready to abort and circle

## Notes & gotchas
- **Verify** the marks against the chart — mistaking one buoy for another causes many groundings.
- If unsure, slow down, hold off, or wait for the tide/daylight. There's no prize for a risky entry.
