# Log Keeping

- **Category:** Navigation & watch
- **Style:** DO–CONFIRM
- **When to use:** Regularly on passage (hourly, or on any change).
- **Crew:** Watch on duty.

> Killer items are shown in **bold**.

## Record at each log entry (hourly typical)
- [ ] Time
- [ ] **Position (lat/long or fix) — and log the source (GPS/DR/fix)**
- [ ] Course steered (COG) and speed (SOG); log reading
- [ ] Wind direction and strength
- [ ] Barometric pressure (note the trend)
- [ ] Sail plan / engine status
- [ ] Sea state and visibility

## Also log any event
- [ ] Course or sail changes
- [ ] Traffic and actions taken
- [ ] Weather changes / forecast received
- [ ] Watch handovers, crew changes
- [ ] Any incident, fault, or near-miss

## Why it matters
- [ ] **A running log lets you fall back on DR if electronics fail**
- [ ] Provides a legal record and aids search-and-rescue
- [ ] Builds situational awareness and hands over context between watches

## Notes & gotchas
- Log the **pressure trend** — a fast fall is an early warning of bad weather.
- Keep it up even when electronics work; it's your backup when they don't.
