# Night Sailing Preparation

- **Category:** Navigation & watch
- **Style:** DO–CONFIRM
- **When to use:** Before darkness falls on passage.
- **Crew:** All hands, before sunset.

> Killer items are shown in **bold**. Prepare while there's still light.

## Before dark
- [ ] **Navigation lights tested and switched on before sunset**
- [ ] **Reef down / reduce sail proactively** — easier now than in the dark
- [ ] Deck tidied; lines coiled; nothing to trip over
- [ ] Torches/headtorches (with **red night-vision mode**) ready and to hand
- [ ] Harnesses and jackstays rigged; **clip-on-at-night rule stated**

## Crew & comfort
- [ ] Watch system confirmed; crew rested/fed before their watch
- [ ] Warm and waterproof layers on
- [ ] Hot food/drinks prepared in flasks
- [ ] Seasickness meds if needed

## Navigation
- [ ] Position fixed and logged; plot the night's route
- [ ] Identify lights/marks expected and their characteristics
- [ ] Dim instrument/chartplotter screens to preserve night vision
- [ ] AIS/radar on; guard zones set

## Confirm
- [ ] **A competent watch on deck; standing orders clear (when to call skipper)**
- [ ] Everyone knows where safety gear is

## Notes & gotchas
- Protect night vision: red light only, screens dimmed, no white lights on deck.
- Reef before dark even if it seems unnecessary — you'll be glad at 0300.
