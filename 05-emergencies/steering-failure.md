# Steering Failure

- **Category:** Emergencies
- **Style:** READ–DO
- **When to use:** Loss of steering (wheel/cable, quadrant, or rudder).
- **Crew:** All hands.

> Killer items are shown in **bold**.

## Assess
1. [ ] Note position, traffic, and hazards; **depower sails to slow the boat**
2. [ ] Determine the failure:
   - Cable/quadrant/linkage (mechanism)
   - Rudder stock / bearing / lost rudder (structural)

## Regain control
- [ ] **Rig the emergency tiller** onto the rudder stock (know where it stows)
- [ ] If linkage failed but rudder is OK, steer directly via emergency tiller
- [ ] If rudder is lost/jammed:
  - Balance sails to steer (trim main vs. headsail)
  - Rig a **drogue/warp bridle from the stern quarters** to steer with
  - Use an oar/floorboard lashed as a steering board if needed

## Manage the situation
- [ ] Slow down; head for sea room or a port of refuge
- [ ] **PAN-PAN (or Mayday if in danger)** with position if you can't steer safely
- [ ] Consider anchoring if near a hazard and in shallow enough water

## After
- [ ] Inspect and attempt a repair when safe
- [ ] Log the event

## Notes & gotchas
- Practice fitting the emergency tiller *before* you ever need it — access is
  often awkward under load.
- Sail balance alone can hold a course on many boats; ease the main to bear away.
