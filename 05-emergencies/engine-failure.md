# Engine Failure

- **Category:** Emergencies
- **Style:** READ–DO
- **When to use:** Engine stops or won't start when needed.
- **Crew:** Skipper + one.

> Killer items are shown in **bold**.

## Immediate seamanship
1. [ ] **Assess sea room and hazards; be ready to sail or anchor**
2. [ ] If near danger, set sail or drop the anchor to hold position

## Diagnose (the common causes)
- [ ] **Fuel:** enough in tank? Fuel cock open? Air in system (bleed)? Blocked filter?
- [ ] **Air/cooling:** raw-water intake open? Weed/plastic on the intake? Impeller OK? Overheat alarm?
- [ ] **Electrical:** battery/isolator on? Kill-cord/stop control reset? Loose terminal?
- [ ] **Fouled prop:** line or weed around the propeller (check before restart)

## Act
- [ ] Clear/replace the offending item (bleed fuel, change filter, change impeller)
- [ ] **Do not re-engage gear if a line may be around the prop** — check first
- [ ] If overheating: stop, let cool, find the cooling fault before restarting

## If not resolved
- [ ] Continue under sail toward a safe destination
- [ ] Call for a tow (PAN-PAN) if unable to make port safely

## After
- [ ] Log symptoms and the fix
- [ ] Restock consumables used (filters, impeller)

## Notes & gotchas
- Most diesel failures are fuel-related: dirty filter, air, or empty/contaminated tank.
- Carry and know how to fit a spare impeller and fuel filter.
