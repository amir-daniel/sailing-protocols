# Grounding

- **Category:** Emergencies
- **Style:** READ–DO
- **When to use:** The boat has run aground / touched bottom.
- **Crew:** All hands.

> Killer items are shown in **bold**.

## Immediate assessment
1. [ ] **Check for hull damage and water ingress first** (see flooding)
2. [ ] Note the state of tide — **rising or falling?** (critical to your plan)
3. [ ] Determine bottom type (mud/sand/rock) and depth around the boat

## If tide is rising (or soft grounding)
- [ ] **Heel the boat to reduce draft** (crew weight out, boom swung out, halyard to a dinghy/anchor)
- [ ] Motor off astern the way you came, if no damage and prop is clear
- [ ] Kedge off: lay an anchor into deep water and winch the boat toward it

## If tide is falling
- [ ] Try to get off **immediately** before you dry out
- [ ] If you'll dry out: **prepare to take the ground safely** — support the boat,
      protect the hull, and lay an anchor to deep water for the refloat
- [ ] Reduce risk of falling over: lean toward safe/upright side

## Call for help
- [ ] **PAN-PAN (or Mayday if holed / in danger)** with position
- [ ] Accept a tow only with a properly secured line and agreed terms

## After
- [ ] Re-check for damage once afloat; monitor the bilge
- [ ] Log the event and note the hazard's position

## Notes & gotchas
- The tide state dictates everything: rising = time is on your side; falling = act now.
- On a lee shore in waves, grounding is serious — call early.
