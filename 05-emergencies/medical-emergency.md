# Medical Emergency

- **Category:** Emergencies
- **Style:** READ–DO
- **When to use:** Serious injury or illness aboard.
- **Crew:** All hands; assign roles.

> Killer items are shown in **bold**. Not a substitute for first-aid training.

## Take charge & assess (primary survey)
1. [ ] **Ensure the scene is safe** for rescuers (clip on, secure the boat)
2. [ ] Assess the casualty — **DR ABC**:
   - **D**anger, **R**esponse
   - **A**irway — open and clear
   - **B**reathing — look, listen, feel
   - **C**irculation — pulse, major bleeding
3. [ ] **Control catastrophic bleeding immediately** (direct pressure)
4. [ ] Start **CPR** if not breathing normally

## Stabilise
- [ ] Treat life threats first: bleeding, airway, breathing
- [ ] Immobilise suspected spinal/fracture injuries
- [ ] Keep the casualty warm; treat for shock; reassure
- [ ] Record observations, times, and any medication given

## Get help
- [ ] **Call for medical advice: DSC + PAN-PAN, or Mayday if life-threatening**
- [ ] Give casualty's condition, vitals, and your position
- [ ] Request evacuation / diversion if advised
- [ ] Prepare for helicopter/lifeboat transfer if arranged

## After
- [ ] Continue monitoring; keep records for the medics
- [ ] Support the rest of the crew
- [ ] Log the event

## Notes & gotchas
- Carry a comprehensive first-aid kit and a offshore medical guide; know your
  crew's pre-existing conditions and medications before departure.
- On VHF you can request medical advice without it being a full distress.
