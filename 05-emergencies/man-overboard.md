# Man Overboard (MOB)

- **Category:** Emergencies
- **Style:** READ–DO
- **When to use:** The instant a person goes over the side.
- **Crew:** All hands.

> Killer items are shown in **bold**. Act immediately — seconds matter.

## Immediate actions (first 10 seconds)
1. [ ] **Shout "MAN OVERBOARD!"** to alert all crew
2. [ ] **Throw flotation** (danbuoy, horseshoe, cushion) toward the casualty
3. [ ] **Press the MOB button** on the GPS/chartplotter to mark position
4. [ ] **Assign a dedicated spotter** to point at the casualty and never look away

## Manoeuvre back
- [ ] Note wind direction; begin return (e.g. tack/gybe, or motor)
- [ ] Start the engine if safe; **check for lines in the water before engaging**
- [ ] Approach from downwind, stopping alongside with boat stopped

## Alert (if not quickly recovered)
- [ ] Send **DSC distress** and broadcast **Mayday** on VHF Ch 16
- [ ] Give position, "man overboard", number in water, description
- [ ] Detail crew to help and, if needed, deploy additional resources

## Recovery
- [ ] Establish and maintain contact; get a line/lifesling to the casualty
- [ ] **Do not put a rescuer in the water except as a last resort, tethered**
- [ ] Recover using lifesling/halyard, boarding ladder, or throwing strop
- [ ] Bring aboard **horizontally if possible** (reduces post-immersion collapse)

## After recovery
- [ ] Treat for hypothermia, shock, and any injuries; monitor breathing
- [ ] Keep casualty warm and still; seek medical advice via VHF
- [ ] Update/cancel the Mayday as appropriate
- [ ] Log the event and debrief crew

## Notes & gotchas
- The spotter's only job is to point — assign it immediately and do not reassign.
- Recovering a heavy, cold casualty from the water is the hardest part: rig a
  mechanical advantage (halyard + winch, lifesling) early.
