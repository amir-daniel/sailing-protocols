# Gas Leak (LPG)

- **Category:** Emergencies
- **Style:** READ–DO
- **When to use:** Smell of gas, gas alarm, or suspected LPG leak.
- **Crew:** All hands.

> Killer items are shown in **bold**. LPG is heavier than air and pools in the bilge — explosion risk.

## Immediate
1. [ ] **NO flames, no sparks, no switches** — do not light the stove, strike a match,
       or operate electrical switches (a spark can ignite pooled gas)
2. [ ] **Turn off the gas at the bottle and at the solenoid**
3. [ ] Extinguish all naked flames (stove, cigarettes)

## Ventilate & clear
- [ ] **Open hatches and ventilate; gas pools low — it won't just blow away**
- [ ] Manually pump/bail the bilge area to help clear heavy vapour if safe
- [ ] Get crew into fresh air on deck

## Find & fix
- [ ] Check the likely sources: hose, connections, regulator, appliance tap left on
- [ ] Leak-test connections with **soapy water** (bubbles show the leak) — never a flame
- [ ] Do not restore gas until the leak is found and fixed and the boat is clear

## If ignition risk remains / can't clear
- [ ] Keep everyone on deck, upwind
- [ ] Be ready for fire (see fire checklist); consider calling for help

## Notes & gotchas
- Because LPG sinks, a leak collects in the bilge where a bilge-pump switch or
  starter can ignite it — that's why **no switches** is the first rule.
- Keep the gas bottle in a self-draining locker vented overboard; turn gas off at
  the bottle when not cooking.
