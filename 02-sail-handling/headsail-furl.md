# Headsail Hoist / Furl (roller furling genoa)

- **Category:** Sail handling
- **Style:** DO–CONFIRM
- **When to use:** Deploying or stowing a roller-furling headsail.
- **Crew:** One in cockpit (can be single-handed).

> Killer items are shown in **bold**.

## Unfurl / deploy
- [ ] Bear off to a reach to reduce load and flogging
- [ ] Ease the furling line, keeping **light tension so it doesn't overrun**
- [ ] Haul the leeward sheet to draw the sail out
- [ ] Trim to course; set the sheet lead / car position
- [ ] Cleat the furling line with a couple of turns retained

## Reef (partial furl)
- [ ] Ease the sheet slightly
- [ ] Take in furling line to desired size
- [ ] **Move the sheet car forward to keep the foot/leech balanced**
- [ ] Re-trim

## Furl away / stow
- [ ] Head onto a reach; ease the sheet so sail depowers
- [ ] Haul the furling line steadily, keeping **tension on the lazy sheet** to get a tight furl
- [ ] Furl fully; **finish with the UV strip on the outside**
- [ ] Cleat furling line firmly; tidy sheets

## Notes & gotchas
- A loose furl in a blow can unroll and overpower the boat — furl tight and cleat hard.
- Never force a jammed furler; find the riding turn or override first.
