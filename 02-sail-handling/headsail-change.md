# Headsail Change (hank-on / twin-groove foil)

- **Category:** Sail handling
- **Style:** READ–DO
- **When to use:** Changing to a smaller/larger headsail as wind changes.
- **Crew:** Helm + one or two on foredeck.

> Killer items are shown in **bold**. Foredeck work — clip on.

## Prepare
- [ ] **Crew on foredeck clipped on to jackstays**
- [ ] Bring new sail forward in its bag, tied to the boat
- [ ] Ease boat onto a reach for a stable, flatter foredeck

## Change (bald-headed method, hank-on)
1. [ ] Hank on new sail below the old sail's bottom hank (if room) or ready alongside
2. [ ] Drop old sail; **secure it to the deck immediately so it can't go overboard**
3. [ ] Unhank old sail; hank on new sail from tack up, in order
4. [ ] Move the halyard and sheets to the new sail
5. [ ] **Confirm hanks are in order and not crossed; tack shackle locked**

## Hoist & trim
- [ ] Hoist new sail; tension halyard
- [ ] Set sheet-car position for the new sail size
- [ ] Bear away, trim, resume course
- [ ] Bag and stow the old sail below

## Notes & gotchas
- Never let a dropped sail hang over the side — it can fill with water and
  drag someone or damage the boat.
- Twin-groove foils allow a hoist-before-drop; watch for two luffs jamming.
