# Reefing the Mainsail (slab / single-line)

- **Category:** Sail handling
- **Style:** READ–DO
- **When to use:** Wind building; "reef early" — when you first think about it.
- **Crew:** Helm + one on mast/cockpit.

> Killer items are shown in **bold**.

## Decide
- [ ] **Reef early — before it becomes hard/dangerous.** If in doubt, reef.
- [ ] Choose reef number for the expected wind

## Prepare
- [ ] Head up to reduce load; ease mainsheet and vang
- [ ] Take up on the topping lift (or set boom on rigid vang)
- [ ] Ensure reefing lines are free to run

## Take the reef
1. [ ] Ease the main halyard to the marked point for this reef
2. [ ] **Hook / secure the luff reef cringle at the tack (gooseneck)**
3. [ ] Re-tension and cleat the main halyard
4. [ ] Haul the leech reef line until the clew cringle is down to the boom
5. [ ] Cleat/winch the reef line firmly
6. [ ] Ease the topping lift back off

## Confirm & tidy
- [ ] **Confirm both tack and clew are secured before loading the sail**
- [ ] Trim mainsheet/vang; bear away onto course
- [ ] Tidy loose sail with reef ties (do not tie to the boom under load)
- [ ] Coil and stow tails

## Notes & gotchas
- Tack first, then clew — a loaded clew with an unsecured tack can tear the sail.
- Reef ties are for tidiness only; the tack and clew carry the load.
