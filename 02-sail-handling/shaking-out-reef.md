# Shaking Out a Reef

- **Category:** Sail handling
- **Style:** DO–CONFIRM
- **When to use:** Wind eased and settled; ready to carry more sail.
- **Crew:** Helm + one on mast/cockpit.

> Killer items are shown in **bold**.

## Decide
- [ ] Confirm the lull is settled, not a temporary gap
- [ ] Shake out one reef at a time

## Prepare
- [ ] Head up to reduce load; ease mainsheet and vang
- [ ] Take up on the topping lift (or set rigid vang)
- [ ] Undo any reef ties in the sail **(all of them — none left tied)**

## Shake out
1. [ ] Ease the leech reef line
2. [ ] Ease the main halyard slightly to free the tack cringle
3. [ ] **Unhook / release the luff tack cringle at the gooseneck**
4. [ ] Hoist the main halyard back to full; tension the luff
5. [ ] Cleat/lock the halyard

## Confirm & tidy
- [ ] **Confirm no reef ties remain in the sail before loading it**
- [ ] Ease topping lift back off
- [ ] Trim, bear away onto course
- [ ] Tidy and coil reef line and halyard tails

## Notes & gotchas
- A forgotten reef tie left in will tear the sail when it fills — check the
  full foot before hoisting.
