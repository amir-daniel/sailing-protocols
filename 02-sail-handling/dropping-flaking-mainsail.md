# Dropping & Flaking the Mainsail

- **Category:** Sail handling
- **Style:** DO–CONFIRM
- **When to use:** Approaching harbour / done sailing / switching to engine.
- **Crew:** Helm + one on the sail.

> Killer items are shown in **bold**.

## Prepare
- [ ] Clear area to motor; **engine started and in gear before dropping (steerage)**
- [ ] Set topping lift / rigid vang to support the boom
- [ ] Ready sail ties within reach
- [ ] Free the main halyard, ensure it will run

## Drop
- [ ] **Turn head-to-wind to depower the sail**
- [ ] Ease mainsheet and vang
- [ ] Lower the halyard steadily; crew gathers sail onto boom
- [ ] Flake sail side to side over the boom (or feed into stackpack)

## Secure
- [ ] Sail ties on at intervals; snug but not crushing battens
- [ ] **Confirm halyard secured and won't hoist accidentally**
- [ ] Ease/adjust topping lift so boom sits at working height
- [ ] Fit sail cover if finished for the day

## Notes & gotchas
- Head-to-wind keeps the sail from filling and dumping into the water.
- Watch fingers between sail/slides and the mast track while lowering.
