# Weather & Tide Review

- **Category:** Pre-departure
- **Style:** DO–CONFIRM
- **When to use:** Immediately before departure (in addition to passage planning).
- **Crew:** Skipper / navigator.

> Killer items are shown in **bold**.

## Weather
- [ ] Get the **latest** forecast (don't rely on yesterday's)
- [ ] **Check for any gale/storm warnings covering your window**
- [ ] Note wind direction & strength vs. your course (beat/reach/run)
- [ ] Note expected changes/fronts and their timing
- [ ] Check visibility (fog risk) and sea state
- [ ] Barometer reading and trend noted

## Tides
- [ ] Times of HW/LW for departure, key gates, and arrival
- [ ] **Direction and rate of tidal streams along the route**
- [ ] Plan to carry a fair tide / make critical gates in the window
- [ ] Confirm sufficient depth (height of tide) at shallow points and berth
- [ ] Check for spring/neap effect on rates and range

## Decision
- [ ] **Go / no-go decision: does the window suit the boat and crew?**
- [ ] Identify the bail-out plan if conditions deteriorate

## Notes & gotchas
- Wind-against-tide creates steep, dangerous seas — check whether they oppose over your route.
- If the forecast and your gut disagree, respect the doubt; there's always another day.
