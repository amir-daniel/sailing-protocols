# Heaving-To

- **Category:** Maneuvers
- **Style:** DO–CONFIRM
- **When to use:** To stop/slow and stabilise — rest, reef, wait, or ride out weather.
- **Crew:** Helm + one (can be single-handed).

> Killer items are shown in **bold**.

## Execute (from close-hauled)
1. [ ] **Tack without releasing the headsail sheet** — leave the jib backed
2. [ ] As the boat comes through, do not touch the jib sheet
3. [ ] Once on the new tack, **turn the helm to leeward (away from the wind) and lash it**
4. [ ] Ease the mainsheet until the boat settles and stalls

## Trim to balance
- [ ] Adjust main and helm so the boat lies roughly 50–60° off the wind, drifting slowly
- [ ] Reduce sail (reef) if overpowered while hove-to
- [ ] Confirm a stable, comfortable slick to windward forming

## Confirm
- [ ] **Check drift direction for sea room / hazards to leeward**
- [ ] Monitor position; the boat will make slow leeway
- [ ] Keep a lookout even while "parked"

## Notes & gotchas
- Every boat heaves-to differently — practise in moderate conditions to learn yours.
- Great for a lunch stop, reefing, or a break; also a valid heavy-weather tactic.
