# Anchoring

- **Category:** Maneuvers
- **Style:** DO–CONFIRM
- **When to use:** Anchoring for a lunch stop or overnight.
- **Crew:** Helm + one on the bow.

> Killer items are shown in **bold**.

## Choose the spot
- [ ] Check chart: depth, **holding type (sand/mud good; weed/rock poor)**, hazards
- [ ] Assess shelter from wind and swell (now and forecast)
- [ ] Note swinging room vs. other boats and the shore
- [ ] Calculate scope: **at least 4:1 (chain) / 6:1 (rope) on max depth incl. tide**

## Set the anchor
1. [ ] Ready the anchor; confirm the bitter end is **made fast to the boat**
2. [ ] Approach slowly **into wind/tide**; stop over the chosen spot
3. [ ] Lower (don't dump) the anchor as the boat begins to drift back
4. [ ] Pay out the calculated scope as you fall back
5. [ ] Snub the rode; **motor gently astern to dig in and confirm it's holding**

## Confirm & monitor
- [ ] Take transits/bearings or set a **GPS anchor-drag alarm**
- [ ] Rig a snubber/bridle (chain) to reduce snatch load
- [ ] Show anchor ball (day) / anchor light (night)
- [ ] **Check the set again after it settles; re-check if wind shifts**

## Notes & gotchas
- Reversing gently to set the anchor is the step people skip — and it's why they drag.
- Allow for tidal rise when calculating both scope and swinging depth.
