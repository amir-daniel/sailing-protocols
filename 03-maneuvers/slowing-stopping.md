# Slowing, Stopping & Ferry Gliding

- **Category:** Maneuvers
- **Style:** DO–CONFIRM
- **When to use:** Controlling speed/position — approaching berths, buoys, locks, or holding station.
- **Crew:** Helm + crew as needed.

> Killer items are shown in **bold**.

## Slowing / stopping under sail
- [ ] **Head up toward the wind to spill power and slow down**
- [ ] Ease sheets to depower the sails
- [ ] Backing the headsail (holding it aback) helps stop / turn the bow
- [ ] To stop completely, come head-to-wind and let sails flog (lose steerage — plan ahead)

## Slowing / stopping under power
- [ ] Reduce throttle early; way carries a long time on the water
- [ ] **Use a burst of astern to stop; expect prop walk to swing the stern**
- [ ] Know your prop-walk direction (which way the stern kicks in reverse)

## Ferry gliding (holding position across a current)
- [ ] Point the bow **up-current** at an angle
- [ ] Adjust engine revs and angle so the boat tracks sideways with little forward motion
- [ ] Use it to hold station or move laterally onto a pontoon/lock wall

## Controlling in close quarters
- [ ] Go **"as slow as possible, as fast as necessary"** for steerage
- [ ] Keep steerage way on — a rudder needs water flow to work
- [ ] Have lines and fenders ready before you commit

## Notes & gotchas
- A sailboat has no brakes — plan your stopping distance well in advance.
- In reverse, the boat steers sluggishly and prop walk dominates at low speed; practise it.
