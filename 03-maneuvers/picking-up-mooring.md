# Picking Up a Mooring

- **Category:** Maneuvers
- **Style:** DO–CONFIRM
- **When to use:** Coming onto a mooring buoy.
- **Crew:** Helm + one on the bow with boat hook.

> Killer items are shown in **bold**.

## Prepare
- [ ] Confirm the mooring is **for visitors / adequate for your boat's size**
- [ ] Ready boat hook and a bow line led through the fairlead
- [ ] Brief the bow crew and agree hand signals
- [ ] Note wind and tide — plan to approach **into the stronger of the two**

## Approach
1. [ ] Approach slowly against wind/tide so the boat naturally stops at the buoy
2. [ ] Bow crew points at the buoy continuously as it disappears under the bow
3. [ ] **Stop the boat with the buoy just at the bow** — don't overrun it

## Secure
- [ ] Hook the pick-up buoy / strop; thread your line through the ring or take the strop
- [ ] **Make fast to a strong cleat; secure before the boat drifts back**
- [ ] Adjust to a bridle if using two bow cleats
- [ ] Check the load takes up and the line isn't chafing

## Confirm
- [ ] Confirm you're holding; take a transit
- [ ] Set snubber/chafe protection for overnight
- [ ] **Check swinging room relative to neighbours**

## Notes & gotchas
- Approach into the dominant force (often tide in a strong stream, wind in light tide).
- If you miss it, circle round and try again — don't lunge or risk crew going over.
