# Weighing (Raising) Anchor

- **Category:** Maneuvers
- **Style:** DO–CONFIRM
- **When to use:** Leaving an anchorage.
- **Crew:** Helm + one on the bow/windlass.

> Killer items are shown in **bold**.

## Prepare
- [ ] Brief crew; agree hand signals (engine noise/distance)
- [ ] Start engine; **confirm cooling water**
- [ ] Stow the anchor snubber/bridle ready to release
- [ ] Tidy the deck; sort the chain locker so chain will stow

## Weigh
1. [ ] Motor slowly ahead toward the anchor as crew takes in chain
2. [ ] Bow crew signals direction/amount of chain to the helm
3. [ ] Take in until the chain is **"up and down"** (vertical, over the anchor)
4. [ ] **Break the anchor out** — motor gently or let the boat's weight free it
5. [ ] Confirm anchor is off the bottom (boat swings free)

## Recover & stow
- [ ] Raise the anchor to the bow roller; **wash off mud/weed as it comes up**
- [ ] **Secure the anchor with a snubber/pin so it can't run out under way**
- [ ] Confirm the windlass is off and clutch secured
- [ ] Motor clear of the anchorage before making sail

## Fouled anchor
- [ ] If it won't break out, snub the chain and motor slowly over it to trip it
- [ ] If chained on an obstruction, use a **trip line / buoy the anchor** to recover
- [ ] Don't overload the windlass — use the boat's engine/weight, not just the motor

## Notes & gotchas
- Drive up to the anchor; don't drag the boat forward on the windlass — it's not a winch for the hull.
- Secure the anchor properly for sea; a self-launching anchor running out under way is dangerous.
