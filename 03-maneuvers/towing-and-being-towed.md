# Towing & Being Towed

- **Category:** Maneuvers
- **Style:** READ–DO
- **When to use:** Giving a tow to, or accepting a tow from, another vessel.
- **Crew:** All hands; skipper coordinates.

> Killer items are shown in **bold**. High loads — keep clear of lines under tension.

## Agree first
- [ ] **Agree terms before connecting** (salvage vs. assistance can have legal/cost implications)
- [ ] Agree signals, speed, and the destination
- [ ] Both crews in lifejackets; brief everyone

## Rigging the tow
- [ ] Use a **long, strong warp with some stretch** (nylon) to absorb snatch loads
- [ ] Spread load with a **bridle** to strong points (not a single cleat if avoidable)
- [ ] **Protect against chafe** at fairleads (the line will saw through under load)
- [ ] Adjust tow length so both vessels ride the **same part of the wave pattern** (crest-to-crest)

## Under tow
- [ ] Start slowly; **take up the load gradually — no sudden snatch**
- [ ] Steer to follow the tow; keep the towline out of the water/prop
- [ ] **Keep everyone clear of the towline** — if it parts it recoils dangerously
- [ ] Have a **knife/means to cut the tow instantly** at both ends
- [ ] Reduce speed for turns and in waves

## Casting off
- [ ] Slow down; ease the load before releasing
- [ ] Recover the line clear of the propeller

## Notes & gotchas
- A parting towline under load is lethal — nobody stands in line with it.
- Keep speed moderate; overpowering the tow risks broaching the towed boat.
