# Tacking

- **Category:** Maneuvers
- **Style:** DO–CONFIRM
- **When to use:** Turning the bow through the wind to change tack.
- **Crew:** Helm + one on the sheets (can be single-handed).

> Killer items are shown in **bold**.

## Prepare
- [ ] **Check for traffic and hazards on the new tack**
- [ ] Ensure enough boat speed to carry through the wind
- [ ] Ready the new (lazy) sheet; one turn on the winch
- [ ] Call **"Ready about?"** — crew confirms **"Ready!"**

## Execute
1. [ ] Call **"Lee-oh"** / "Tacking" and turn smoothly toward the wind
2. [ ] As the headsail backs, release the old sheet cleanly
3. [ ] Haul in the new sheet quickly; winch to trim
4. [ ] Steer onto the new close-hauled course; don't over-turn

## Confirm
- [ ] Trim headsail and main for the new tack
- [ ] **Confirm old sheet is clear and won't foul**
- [ ] Settle onto course; check boat speed rebuilds

## Notes & gotchas
- Too slow into the tack = caught "in irons"; keep speed up before turning.
- Take the lazy sheet's slack in early so the winch job is quick.
