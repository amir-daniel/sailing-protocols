# Gybing

- **Category:** Maneuvers
- **Style:** DO–CONFIRM
- **When to use:** Turning the stern through the wind (downwind change).
- **Crew:** Helm + one on the mainsheet.

> Killer items are shown in **bold**. The boom crosses under load — control it.

## Prepare
- [ ] **Check for traffic and hazards on the new gybe**
- [ ] **Warn all crew: "heads down" — clear of the mainsheet and boom path**
- [ ] Take in the mainsheet to centre the boom (controlled gybe)
- [ ] Ready the new headsail sheet / prepare the pole if flying a spinnaker
- [ ] Call **"Ready to gybe?"** — crew confirms **"Ready!"**

## Execute
1. [ ] Call **"Gybe-oh"** and bear away smoothly through dead downwind
2. [ ] As the stern passes the wind, **control the boom across — do not let it slam**
3. [ ] Ease the mainsheet immediately on the new side
4. [ ] Bring the headsail across; trim on the new side

## Confirm
- [ ] Steer onto the new course; ease vang/sheet to correct trim
- [ ] Check the preventer is re-rigged if used
- [ ] **Confirm no crew or lines in the boom's swing path**

## Notes & gotchas
- An uncontrolled "crash gybe" can injure crew and damage the rig — always sheet
  in first and control the boom across.
- Rig a **boom preventer** downwind to avoid accidental gybes.
