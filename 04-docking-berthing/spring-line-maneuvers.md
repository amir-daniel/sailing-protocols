# Spring-Line Maneuvers

- **Category:** Docking & berthing
- **Style:** READ–DO
- **When to use:** Leaving/berthing in tight spots or when wind/tide pins you on.
- **Crew:** Helm + one on the spring.

> Killer items are shown in **bold**.

## Concept
- A **spring** is a line led fore-and-aft that, worked against engine thrust,
  levers the bow or stern in or out. It lets you turn the boat in its own length.

## Springing the stern out (to drive off forward)
1. [ ] Rig a **bow spring** (from bow, back to a dock cleat) as a slip line
2. [ ] Put fenders at the bow shoulder (the pivot/contact point)
3. [ ] **Take in all other lines**
4. [ ] Motor **gently ahead against the spring** — the stern swings out
5. [ ] When angled enough, slip the spring and reverse out

## Springing the bow out (to drive off astern)
1. [ ] Rig a **stern spring** (from stern, forward to a dock cleat) as a slip line
2. [ ] Fenders at the stern shoulder
3. [ ] **Take in all other lines**
4. [ ] Motor **gently astern against the spring** — the bow swings out
5. [ ] Slip the spring and drive off forward

## Confirm
- [ ] **Confirm the spring is a slip line you can release from aboard**
- [ ] Recover spring and fenders once clear
- [ ] Watch the pivoting end doesn't contact the dock unfendered

## Notes & gotchas
- Keep engine revs low — the spring does the work, not brute force.
- Good fendering at the pivot point is what protects the hull; place it precisely.
