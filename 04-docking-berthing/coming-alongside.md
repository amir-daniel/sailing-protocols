# Arrival / Coming Alongside

- **Category:** Docking & berthing
- **Style:** DO–CONFIRM
- **When to use:** Coming alongside a pontoon, wall, or raft.
- **Crew:** Helm + dock crew.

> Killer items are shown in **bold**.

## Prepare
- [ ] Call ahead / confirm the berth and which side to
- [ ] **Rig fenders on the docking side at the right height; lines ready**
- [ ] Prepare bow line, stern line, and springs, led outside everything
- [ ] Brief crew: who steps off (only when safe), which line first
- [ ] **Assess wind/tide** — plan to approach against the stronger

## Approach
1. [ ] Approach slowly at a shallow angle against wind/tide
2. [ ] Control speed — **"as slow as possible, as fast as necessary"**
3. [ ] Bring the boat parallel and stop alongside
4. [ ] **Step ashore calmly — never jump; don't fend with hands/legs**

## Secure
- [ ] Get a spring or the first line on to stop movement
- [ ] Attach bow, stern, and springs; adjust for tide range
- [ ] Set fenders correctly; add spring/chafe protection
- [ ] **Allow slack for tidal rise/fall if drying or big range**

## Confirm
- [ ] Engine off; shore power / checks as needed
- [ ] Lines tidy, chafe protected
- [ ] Fenders adjusted to actual contact points

## Notes & gotchas
- Approach into the strongest of wind/tide for the most control at low speed.
- A midships spring on first is the easiest single line to control the boat.
