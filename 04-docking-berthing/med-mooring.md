# Med Mooring (stern-to / bows-to)

- **Category:** Docking & berthing
- **Style:** READ–DO
- **When to use:** Mediterranean-style berthing to a quay with an anchor or lazy line.
- **Crew:** Helm + bow + stern crew (min 3 helpful).

> Killer items are shown in **bold**.

## Prepare
- [ ] Decide **stern-to** (easier boarding) or **bows-to** (more privacy/prop protection)
- [ ] Rig fenders **both sides**; ready two stern (or bow) lines to the quay
- [ ] Ready the anchor with plenty of scope, **or** locate the lazy line
- [ ] Brief crew and agree signals; note wind/cross-wind

## Approach & drop (anchor method, stern-to)
1. [ ] Line up perpendicular to the quay, allowing for cross-wind
2. [ ] **Drop the anchor well out (≈4–5 boat lengths), paying out chain as you back in**
3. [ ] Reverse toward the quay, steering with bursts of power
4. [ ] Keep enough tension on the anchor chain to stay straight

## Secure
- [ ] Get **stern lines ashore to the quay** and made fast
- [ ] Tension the anchor to hold the bow off; adjust stern lines
- [ ] **Keep the boat off the quay — never let the stern/rudder hit** 
- [ ] Rig a passerelle/plank for boarding

## Lazy-line variant
- [ ] Pick up the lazy line at the quay, **walk it forward and make it fast at the bow**
- [ ] Tension it to hold the bow off in place of an anchor

## Confirm
- [ ] Adjust all lines for even load and clearance
- [ ] Check neighbours' anchors aren't crossed with yours
- [ ] Fenders positioned for actual contact with neighbours

## Notes & gotchas
- Crossed anchors are the classic Med-mooring mess — drop straight and note where neighbours' chains lead.
- Protect the rudder/prop: stop before the stern touches; use a long stern line rather than backing hard onto the quay.
