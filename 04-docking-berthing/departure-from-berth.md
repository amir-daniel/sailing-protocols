# Departure from Berth

- **Category:** Docking & berthing
- **Style:** DO–CONFIRM
- **When to use:** Leaving a marina berth or alongside.
- **Crew:** Helm + dock crew.

> Killer items are shown in **bold**.

## Before slipping
- [ ] Complete the pre-start/engine checks (see engine checklist)
- [ ] **Assess wind and tide direction and strength** at the berth
- [ ] Brief crew: order of lines, who steps off/on, fending
- [ ] Fenders placed for the planned exit and any pivot
- [ ] Agree the plan (drive off bow/stern, spring off, ferry glide)

## Slip the lines
- [ ] Engine running, **cooling water confirmed**, gear tested ahead/astern
- [ ] Remove unneeded lines; keep a slip line / spring for control
- [ ] **Keep hands and fingers clear of cleats and gaps between hull and pontoon**
- [ ] Use a spring to swing the bow/stern out if wind/tide pins you on

## Depart
- [ ] Let go the last line only when the exit path is clear
- [ ] Drive off smoothly; watch the stern swings toward the pontoon
- [ ] **Recover all lines and fenders once clear** (before they foul the prop)

## Confirm
- [ ] Lines coiled, fenders stowed
- [ ] Clear of the marina; settle onto course

## Notes & gotchas
- A slip line (rove back to the boat) lets you release from aboard — no one gets left on the dock.
- Wind/tide pinning you on? Use a spring to lever off rather than powering against it.
