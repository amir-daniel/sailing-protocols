# Right of Way & Steering Rules (COLREGs)

- **Category:** COLREGs quick-reference
- **Style:** Reference
- **When to use:** Understanding who gives way and how to act to avoid collision.

> **This is a memory aid, not the legal text.** Always know the full
> International Regulations for Preventing Collisions at Sea (COLREGs).

## Overriding principles
- **Rule 5 — Lookout:** keep a proper lookout by sight and hearing at all times.
- **Rule 6 — Safe speed:** always proceed at a safe speed for the conditions.
- **Rule 7 — Risk of collision:** if a bearing to another vessel is **steady and
  the range closing, a risk of collision exists**. Assume it does if in doubt.
- **Rule 8 — Action:** any avoiding action must be **early, large, and obvious**.
- **Rule 2 — Responsibility:** nothing excuses neglect of ordinary good seamanship;
  break the rules if needed to avoid immediate collision.

## Give-way ("burdened") vs Stand-on
- **Give-way vessel (Rule 16):** keep clear — act early and substantially.
- **Stand-on vessel (Rule 17):** maintain course and speed, **but take action if
  the give-way vessel isn't acting**, and must act to avoid collision at close quarters.

## Two power-driven vessels
- **Head-on (Rule 14):** both alter course **to starboard**; pass port-to-port.
- **Crossing (Rule 15):** the vessel that has the **other on its own starboard side
  gives way** ("if to starboard red appear, 'tis your duty to keep clear").
- **Overtaking (Rule 13):** the **overtaking vessel keeps clear** of the one ahead —
  regardless of type. Overtaking = approaching from more than 22.5° abaft the beam.

## Sailing vessels (Rule 12) — when both are under sail
1. **Opposite tacks:** the vessel on **port tack gives way** to the one on starboard tack.
2. **Same tack:** the **windward vessel gives way** to the leeward vessel.
3. If a port-tack vessel can't tell the other's tack, assume it must give way.

## Pecking order (Rule 18 — who gives way to whom, generally)
Power-driven gives way to → sailing → engaged in fishing → restricted in ability
to manoeuvre → not under command. (Roughly least-manoeuvrable has priority.)

- **A sailing vessel gives way to:** NUC, RAM, and vessels engaged in fishing.
- **In a narrow channel (Rule 9):** keep to starboard; **don't impede** vessels that
  can only navigate within the channel. Small craft/sailing must not impede a vessel
  confined to the channel.
- **Traffic Separation Schemes (Rule 10):** join/leave at the ends or cross on a
  **heading as near 90° as practicable**; don't impede lane traffic.

## Notes & gotchas
- "Stand-on" does **not** mean "hold course into a collision" — you must still act
  to avoid one if the other vessel doesn't.
- A big ship may not see you and may be **constrained by draft** — never assume you
  have right of way over a ship in a channel; stay clear.
- Take a compass bearing on approaching vessels; **constant bearing = collision risk**.
