# Navigation Lights (COLREGs)

- **Category:** COLREGs quick-reference
- **Style:** Reference
- **When to use:** Identifying vessels at night / restricted visibility, and
  showing the correct lights yourself.

> Memory aid only — learn the full Rules (20–31) and arcs.

## Light arcs (the basics)
- **Sidelights:** red (port) & green (starboard), each showing **112.5°** from
  ahead to just abaft the beam.
- **Sternlight:** white, **135°** covering the stern.
- **Masthead/steaming light:** white, **225°** forward arc (over the sidelights' arc).
- **All-round light:** white/red/green, **360°**.
- Sidelights + sternlight together = **360°** around the vessel.

## What you'll commonly see
| Vessel | Lights |
| --- | --- |
| **Power-driven underway** (<50m) | Masthead (white, fwd), sidelights, sternlight |
| **Power-driven ≥50m** | **Two** masthead lights (2nd higher & aft), sidelights, sternlight |
| **Sailing vessel underway** | Sidelights + sternlight (**no** masthead steaming light) |
| Sailing — optional | May add **red-over-green** all-round at the masthead |
| **Sail under engine ("motor-sailing")** | Treated as power-driven — show a masthead light + a cone by day |
| **Vessel at anchor** (<50m) | One **all-round white** light (forward); ≥50m shows two |
| **Vessel aground** | Anchor light(s) **plus two all-round red** in a vertical line |

## Special vessels — vertical all-round lights
| Vessel | Lights (top→bottom) |
| --- | --- |
| **Not Under Command (NUC)** | **Red over Red** ("red over red, the captain is dead") |
| **Restricted in Ability to Manoeuvre (RAM)** | **Red / White / Red** |
| **Constrained by Draft** | **Three Red** in a vertical line |
| **Fishing (trawling)** | **Green over White** |
| **Fishing (other)** | **Red over White** ("red over white, fishing at night") |
| **Pilot vessel** | **White over Red** ("white over red, pilot ahead") |
| **Towing (tow >200m)** | Tug: 3 masthead lights + yellow towing light over sternlight |

## Working out aspect
- **See both red & green + white above** → vessel roughly **head-on** (power-driven).
- **See green + white** → you're looking at her **starboard** side.
- **See red + white** → you're looking at her **port** side.
- **See only a white light** → likely a **sternlight** (you're overtaking) or an
  anchored/small vessel — treat with caution.

## Notes & gotchas
- A sailing vessel shows **no** forward white steaming light; if you see one, treat
  her as power-driven (she may be motoring).
- Learn the coloured mnemonics — they're fast recall at 0200 in a shipping lane.
