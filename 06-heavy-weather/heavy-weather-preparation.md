# Heavy-Weather Preparation

- **Category:** Heavy weather
- **Style:** READ–DO
- **When to use:** Bad weather forecast/approaching — prepare BEFORE it hits.
- **Crew:** All hands.

> Killer items are shown in **bold**. Do this while it's still easy.

## Boat & deck
- [ ] **Reef early — reduce sail before the wind builds** (see reefing checklist)
- [ ] Rig storm sails / know where they are; consider setting them early
- [ ] **Rig jackstays; everyone clips on** for any deck work
- [ ] Close and dog all hatches, ports, and the companionway; fit washboards
- [ ] Secure and lash everything on deck (anchor, jerry cans, dinghy, gear)
- [ ] Check bilge pumps work; keep the bilge dry
- [ ] Rig a boom preventer for downwind work

## Below decks
- [ ] Stow and secure all loose gear; heavy items low and locked
- [ ] Prepare easy food/hot drinks in flasks before it gets rough
- [ ] Ready the grab bag, flares, and handheld VHF in a known place

## Crew
- [ ] **Lifejackets and harnesses on; tethers checked**
- [ ] Seasickness meds taken early (before feeling ill)
- [ ] Warm/dry layers on; hydrate and eat now
- [ ] Brief the plan, watch system, and MOB drill; reduce crew on deck

## Navigation
- [ ] Fix position; identify **sea room and nearest ports of refuge**
- [ ] Note hazards, lee shores, and tidal gates to avoid
- [ ] Log position regularly; keep DR going in case of electronics loss

## Notes & gotchas
- Everything is easier before the weather arrives — reef, eat, and clip on early.
- A lee shore is the biggest danger; prioritise sea room.
