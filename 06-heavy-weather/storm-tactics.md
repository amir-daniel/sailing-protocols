# Storm Tactics

- **Category:** Heavy weather
- **Style:** READ–DO
- **When to use:** Managing the boat in a gale/storm at sea.
- **Crew:** All hands; minimise people on deck.

> Killer items are shown in **bold**.

## First principles
- [ ] **Prioritise sea room — get away from lee shores and hazards**
- [ ] Keep the crew safe, warm, and clipped on; rotate short watches
- [ ] Reduce sail to keep the boat balanced and under control (storm canvas)

## Active tactics (choosing by conditions & boat)
- **Heave-to** (see heaving-to): park with reduced/storm sail; good early tactic,
  makes a protective slick to windward.
- **Fore-reaching:** slowly making way to windward under storm sail, actively steered.
- **Running off:** run downwind before the seas under bare poles / small sail,
  **trailing warps or a drogue to slow the boat and prevent surfing/broaching**.
- **Lying to a sea anchor:** hold the bow to the seas from the front (needs sea room to leeward).
- **Lying ahull:** last resort — no sail, helm lashed; **vulnerable to being rolled beam-on**.

## Steering the seas
- [ ] Take the largest breaking seas **slightly on the bow or quarter, not beam-on**
- [ ] Ease the boat over/through crests; avoid burying the bow or broaching
- [ ] Watch for rogue/breaking waves; call them to the helm

## Manage
- [ ] **Keep hatches shut and the boat watertight; monitor the bilge**
- [ ] Conserve crew energy; hot drinks; prevent hypothermia
- [ ] Log position and keep DR; be ready to call for help (PAN-PAN/Mayday)

## Notes & gotchas
- The best tactic depends on your boat, the sea state, and sea room — there is no
  single right answer; avoid lying beam-on to breaking seas.
- A drogue/series drogue deployed early is far easier than in full survival conditions.
