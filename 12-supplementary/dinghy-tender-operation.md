# Dinghy / Tender Operation

- **Category:** Supplementary
- **Style:** DO–CONFIRM
- **When to use:** Using the tender to get ashore / between boat and shore.
- **Crew:** Anyone.

> Killer items are shown in **bold**.

## Before you go
- [ ] **Lifejackets on for everyone** (the tender is where many drownings happen)
- [ ] Don't **overload** — respect the person/weight limit
- [ ] Bailer, painter, and (offshore/night) a torch and handheld VHF/phone aboard
- [ ] Oars/paddle aboard even if using the outboard (backup propulsion)
- [ ] Kill-cord attached to the driver (if outboard fitted)

## Boarding & underway
- [ ] Board one at a time, stepping into the **centre**; keep low
- [ ] Distribute weight evenly; keep crew seated
- [ ] Go at a safe speed; watch for swimmers, lines, and other craft
- [ ] Know the fuel state and range; allow for wind/current pushing you off course

## Returning / stowing
- [ ] Approach the boat/dock into wind/tide; **secure the painter**
- [ ] Lift/tow/stow the tender securely; **remove the bung when stowed** (if applicable)
- [ ] Lock or secure the outboard and tender against theft

## Notes & gotchas
- Cold water and overloading make the tender the most dangerous small boat you'll use —
  lifejackets always, no exceptions, even for a short row.
- A drifting tender that gets away in current is a common (avoidable) emergency — secure the painter.
