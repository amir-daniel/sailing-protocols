# Collision (Aftermath)

- **Category:** Supplementary
- **Style:** READ–DO
- **When to use:** Immediately after a collision with another vessel or object.
- **Crew:** All hands.

> Killer items are shown in **bold**.

## Immediate
1. [ ] **Check all crew for injuries** (yours and, if possible, the other vessel's)
2. [ ] **Check for water ingress / hull damage** (see flooding checklist)
3. [ ] Check for fire/fuel leak; check the rig is standing
4. [ ] Stop the boat / take way off if safe; assess before manoeuvring again

## Safety of life first
- [ ] Render assistance to the other vessel if safe — **you are legally obliged** to
      help persons in danger if you can do so without serious risk to your own vessel
- [ ] If anyone is in danger: **Mayday / PAN-PAN** as appropriate
- [ ] Treat injuries (see medical emergency)

## Manage the vessel
- [ ] Deal with flooding/damage; deploy pumps; plug/brace breaches
- [ ] If seaworthy, proceed to a safe port; if not, prepare accordingly
- [ ] Preserve the rig if damaged (see dismasting)

## Admin (once safe)
- [ ] **Exchange details** with the other vessel (name, owner, insurer, contact)
- [ ] **Record everything:** time, position, conditions, what happened, photos
- [ ] Note witnesses
- [ ] Report to the relevant authority/coastguard/insurer as required

## Notes & gotchas
- Safety of people and the vessel comes first; the paperwork comes after everyone's safe.
- There is a legal duty to render assistance after a collision where you can do so safely.
