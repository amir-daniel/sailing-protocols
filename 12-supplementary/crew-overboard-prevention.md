# Crew-Overboard Prevention

- **Category:** Supplementary
- **Style:** DO–CONFIRM
- **When to use:** Continuously — the best MOB is the one that never happens.
- **Crew:** All hands; skipper enforces.

> Killer items are shown in **bold**. Prevention beats recovery every time.

## Golden rules
- [ ] **"One hand for the boat"** — always hold on when moving about
- [ ] Move about on the **windward side**, low, staying inboard
- [ ] Keep your **centre of gravity low**; don't stand unnecessarily

## Clip-on policy (state it as a standing order)
- [ ] **Clip on with a harness/tether when:** on deck at night, in heavy weather,
      on the foredeck, when reefing, or when alone on watch
- [ ] Jackstays rigged bow-to-stern; clip to them, not to lifelines
- [ ] Clip on **before leaving the cockpit**; stay clipped until back
- [ ] Tether short enough that you **can't go over the side**, not just be dragged

## Equipment
- [ ] Lifejackets with crotch straps, light, and ideally AIS/PLB
- [ ] Guardrails/lifelines checked and sound
- [ ] Non-slip deck; footwear with grip

## Behaviour
- [ ] Pee below or in a bottle — **do not urinate over the side** (a top MOB cause, especially at night, alone, after alcohol)
- [ ] No unnecessary foredeck trips in the dark; plan sail changes early
- [ ] Buddy system at night; tell someone before going forward

## Notes & gotchas
- A large share of MOB fatalities are men, at night, alone, found with flies undone —
  the "pee over the side" rule is deadly serious.
- Recovering a MOB is hard and often fails; preventing it is nearly free.
