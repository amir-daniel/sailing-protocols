# Sea-Sickness Management

- **Category:** Supplementary
- **Style:** DO–CONFIRM
- **When to use:** Prevention before/at the start of a passage; treating an afflicted crew member.
- **Crew:** Anyone.

> Killer items are shown in **bold**.

## Prevention (before it starts)
- [ ] **Take medication early — before departure / before feeling ill** (it doesn't work once you're sick)
- [ ] Rest and hydrate beforehand; avoid a hangover or exhaustion
- [ ] Eat light, plain food; avoid greasy food and excess alcohol
- [ ] Stay warm; get fresh air on deck; watch the horizon
- [ ] Take the helm / a task — being active and focused forward helps
- [ ] Avoid going below, screens, and close cabin work in a seaway

## If someone is seasick
- [ ] **Keep them on deck, clipped on, low in the cockpit, watching the horizon**
- [ ] Keep them warm and hydrated; small sips of water
- [ ] Give a task (steering) if they're able
- [ ] **Watch them closely — a seasick crew member is a MOB risk** (weak, going to the rail)
- [ ] Rotate them out of demanding jobs; let them rest once stable

## Notes & gotchas
- Seasickness causes dangerous **apathy and dehydration** — a badly sick crew member
  becomes a safety liability, so manage it as a safety issue, not just discomfort.
- Never let a seasick person lean over the leeward rail unclipped to be sick.
