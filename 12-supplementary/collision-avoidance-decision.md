# Collision-Avoidance Decision (COLREGs in practice)

- **Category:** Supplementary
- **Style:** DO–CONFIRM
- **When to use:** Another vessel is approaching and you must decide what to do.
- **Crew:** Watch on deck.

> Killer items are shown in **bold**. See also the COLREGs quick-reference.

## Detect
- [ ] **Maintain a proper lookout** — sight and hearing, all round, all the time
- [ ] Note the other vessel: type, aspect (lights/shape), and rough course

## Assess risk
- [ ] **Take a compass bearing on the vessel; watch it over time**
- [ ] **Steady bearing + closing range = risk of collision** — assume risk if in doubt
- [ ] Use AIS/radar (CPA/TCPA) to confirm, but **don't rely on it alone**

## Decide roles
- [ ] Determine give-way vs stand-on (see right-of-way rules)
- [ ] **If you are give-way: act early and substantially** — a big, obvious alteration
- [ ] If you are stand-on: hold course/speed, **but be ready to act if they don't**

## Act
- [ ] Alterations **early, large, and obvious** (a small change won't be seen/understood)
- [ ] Prefer an **alteration of course** to a change of speed where possible
- [ ] Alter to pass **astern** of a crossing vessel, not ahead of it
- [ ] Sound **five short blasts** if their intentions are unclear/you doubt their action
- [ ] **Re-check the bearing after acting** to confirm the risk is gone

## Notes & gotchas
- A big ship travels fast and can't manoeuvre — never rely on right of way over it;
  assume it hasn't seen you and keep well clear.
- One clear, early, obvious alteration beats several small hesitant ones.
