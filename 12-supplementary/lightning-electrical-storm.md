# Lightning / Electrical Storm

- **Category:** Supplementary
- **Style:** READ–DO
- **When to use:** A thunderstorm is approaching or overhead.
- **Crew:** All hands.

> Killer items are shown in **bold**.

## Before it hits
- [ ] Monitor weather; **avoid the storm if you can** (route around it)
- [ ] Reduce sail; prepare for the squall/gusts that accompany storms
- [ ] Everyone in lifejackets; secure the boat (see heavy-weather prep)

## During the storm
- [ ] **Stay low in the cockpit / go below; keep crew off the deck and mast area**
- [ ] **Don't touch metal — stays, shrouds, wheel, rails, mast** (path to ground)
- [ ] Avoid being the highest point; keep hands/body inside the boat
- [ ] Reduce use of electronics; consider **disconnecting/unplugging key electronics**
      and the handheld GPS/VHF (keep a spare handheld in the oven/Faraday-ish spot)
- [ ] Steer to keep control through gusts and wind shifts

## After a strike (if hit)
- [ ] **Check crew for injuries; be ready to perform CPR** (lightning can stop the heart)
- [ ] **Check for hull damage/leaks** — a strike can blow out a through-hull or transducer
- [ ] Check the rig and steering
- [ ] Test electronics, compass, and navigation gear — a strike often **fries electronics
      and can leave the compass unreliable**; navigate with caution/backups
- [ ] Head for port to inspect; report as needed

## Notes & gotchas
- After any strike, distrust your electronics **and** your compass until checked.
- Keep a backup handheld GPS/VHF stored where a strike is less likely to reach it.
