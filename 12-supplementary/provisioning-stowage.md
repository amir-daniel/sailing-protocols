# Provisioning & Stowage (for a passage)

- **Category:** Supplementary
- **Style:** DO–CONFIRM
- **When to use:** Preparing for a passage or extended cruise.
- **Crew:** Skipper + crew.

> Killer items are shown in **bold**.

## Food & water
- [ ] Plan meals for the passage **plus a reserve** for delays
- [ ] **Fresh water: adequate tankage + emergency water in the grab bag**
- [ ] Easy, no-cook / one-pot meals for rough weather
- [ ] Snacks and hot-drink supplies accessible for night watches
- [ ] Note crew dietary needs/allergies

## Stowage
- [ ] **Heavy items stowed low and secured** (keep weight central and low)
- [ ] Everything stowed so nothing flies in a knockdown/rough seas
- [ ] Frequently used items accessible without unpacking lockers
- [ ] Keep a **stowage list** — know where things are without searching
- [ ] Protect against damp; keep dry stores sealed

## Safety-linked provisioning
- [ ] Medications (crew's own + first-aid consumables) topped up
- [ ] Fuel, gas, and water logged against the plan
- [ ] Grab bag food/water checked and in date

## Notes & gotchas
- In a seaway you won't want to cook — plan easy meals so the crew keeps eating.
- Unsecured stores become dangerous missiles in a knockdown; lash it all down.
