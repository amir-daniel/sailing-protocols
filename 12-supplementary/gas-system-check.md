# Gas System Operational Check

- **Category:** Supplementary
- **Style:** DO–CONFIRM
- **When to use:** Routinely (part of pre-departure) and when changing a bottle.
- **Crew:** Skipper or delegated.

> Killer items are shown in **bold**. Routine check — for a *leak emergency* see the gas-leak checklist.

## Bottle & locker
- [ ] Adequate gas in the bottle; a spare secured
- [ ] Bottle stored in a **self-draining locker vented overboard** (not the cabin/bilge)
- [ ] Bottle secured so it can't move at sea

## Connections
- [ ] Regulator and hose in date and not perished/cracked
- [ ] Connections tight; **leak-test with soapy water (never a flame)** — no bubbles
- [ ] Bubble tester / gas alarm (if fitted) working

## Operation
- [ ] Solenoid (if fitted) switches the supply on/off
- [ ] Stove lights cleanly and burns with a steady blue flame
- [ ] **Turn gas off at the bottle when finished cooking and when leaving the boat**

## Notes & gotchas
- LPG is heavier than air; a leak sinks into the bilge — that's why the locker must
  drain **overboard**, not inboard.
- Turning off at the bottle (not just the tap) is the habit that prevents slow leaks.
