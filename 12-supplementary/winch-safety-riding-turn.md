# Winch Safety & Clearing a Riding Turn

- **Category:** Supplementary
- **Style:** DO–CONFIRM
- **When to use:** Any winch work; and when a riding turn (override) jams a winch.
- **Crew:** Anyone on sheets/halyards.

> Killer items are shown in **bold**. Winches generate huge loads — respect them.

## Winch safety
- [ ] **Keep fingers clear of the drum and the gap where line meets the winch**
- [ ] Put on enough turns (usually 3) so the line grips and won't slip under load
- [ ] Load the winch so turns lead **upward** onto the drum (prevents overrides)
- [ ] Never wrap a loaded sheet around your hand; **tail with an open hand**
- [ ] Ease a loaded line **under control** with turns still on — don't just cast it off
- [ ] Keep clothing/hair clear of a spinning drum

## Clearing a riding turn (override)
1. [ ] **Take the load off** the jammed line if possible (use another winch/cleat/sheet
       to a second lead so you can slacken the overridden one)
2. [ ] Once slack, work the crossed turn free by hand
3. [ ] Re-lead the line correctly (fair lead, turns upward) and resume
4. [ ] If badly loaded, rig a **rolling hitch** onto the loaded line to transfer the
       load to another winch, then clear the override

## Notes & gotchas
- A riding turn under full load can't just be pulled free — you must transfer the load
  off it first (second winch / rolling hitch), or you risk crushed fingers.
- Prevent overrides: correct lead angle and turns going up the drum, not piling down.
