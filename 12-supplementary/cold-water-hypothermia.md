# Cold Water & Hypothermia

- **Category:** Supplementary
- **Style:** READ–DO
- **When to use:** Immersion, prolonged cold exposure, or after a MOB recovery.
- **Crew:** All hands; assign care.

> Killer items are shown in **bold**. See also medical emergency and MOB.

## Recognise
- [ ] Early: shivering, cold, clumsy, slurred speech, apathy
- [ ] Worsening: **shivering stops**, confusion, drowsiness, collapse (this is severe)

## Immersion / recovery priorities
- [ ] **Recover a cold casualty horizontally if possible** — sudden vertical lift can
      cause circulatory collapse ("after-drop")
- [ ] **Handle gently; keep them as still as possible** (rough handling can trigger cardiac arrest)
- [ ] Get them out of the wind and water immediately

## Treat
- [ ] **Remove wet clothing; insulate from head to toe**, including underneath them
- [ ] Warm gradually: blankets, dry layers, shelter below; **warm the core, not just extremities**
- [ ] If conscious: warm **sweet** drinks (no alcohol, no caffeine)
- [ ] **Do NOT rub the limbs or apply direct intense heat** to a severely cold casualty
- [ ] Monitor breathing/consciousness; **be ready for CPR** in severe cases
- [ ] Seek medical advice (PAN-PAN/Mayday if severe)

## Notes & gotchas
- "**Not dead until warm and dead**" — continue resuscitation efforts on a severely
  hypothermic casualty; cold can protect the brain, and they can recover.
- After-drop and rough handling kill — move a cold casualty gently and horizontally.
